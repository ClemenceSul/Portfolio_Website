Spotfind

With Spotfind, never lose your treasures again! The device aims to help care-givers and care-receivers. Indeed, items are often misplaced when either party tidies the house, and often leads to great frustration and tension.

User research. To find a solution FOR them.

Multiple interviews were conducted with care-givers to pinpoint their needs. We also observed their experience with an existing item traking device over a week. The care-givers were quite interested by this concept, and helped us refine our product’s requirements.

Device communication throughout the house. With Radio Frequency.

As CTO in my group, I conducted extensive desk research to analyse all the possible technologies available to measure distance between two systems and decided to use radio frequency (RF) as it offered the optimal price to range ratio. Tags emits regular messages to the device, enabling it to locate the position of the item the tag is attached to. The device can be linked to up to 100 tags, which can be stored in the drawer of the dock.

Tailored user experience. Simple and efficient.

On main device's touchscreen, controlled by a Raspberry Pi, the users can manage their tags and launch the search. Spotfinder leads the user through with haptic feedback and the LED range, indicating how far away the tags are located.

Industry Influenced Casing. For a quick assembly.

All casings were modelled in 3D on Fusion with self-tapering screw holes and precise positioning support to ease the assembly process of both devices. The tags were designed with a paper-clip like back, attaching to envelopes, which can slot onto a sticker-like base useful for other objects
