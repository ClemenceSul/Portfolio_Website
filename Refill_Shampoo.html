Refill Shampoo

A sustainability report

Autumn 2021

Some markets are striving to become more sustainable and refillable pouches are gaining popularity for personnal care products such as shampoos. An analysis of The Good Refill pouches reployed by Head & Shoulders from P&G observed this as a sustainable solution but not the most sustainable.

Thorough desk research to analyse the current market

Practical observation of a product’s production

Market research. Through desk research, 4 main forms of shampoo were identified with a varying environmental impact. These were compared throughout their lifespan in order and the solid car or cube of shampoo was found to be the most sustainable, followed by refill pouches like our product.

Product Analysis. To evaluate the sustainability of this product, all elements were taken apart and the various materials used were identified. Overall, these items consume 10.7 MJ of energy and emmits 0.57 kg of CO2 through their lifespan.

System Analysis. The events in the system with the opportunity to improve the system and increase it’s sustainability were identified. Most could be redesigned to ameliorate user experience, others concern the free material ressources available for companies involved in recycling.
