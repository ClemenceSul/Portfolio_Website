Discover Windows

An interactive webapp

Summer 2021

In older generations, computer illiteracy causes disparities, isolation or dependancy. I created this webapp as an easy interactive guide to give the elderly the necessary tools to communicate, understand and use their computers.

Programmed with HTML, CSS and Javascript

Accessible user interface designed for the elderly

Pinpointing the problem. When helping my grandparents, I was alarmed at how confused and frustrated they would get when performing simple actions on their computers. Following further interviews with other seniors, the main issue was identified as a lack of knowledge which hinders their ability to communicate their problems/needs with others and reach solutions.

A concept for users. Aimed to help the elderly, my webapp needed to be tailored to their behavior. After observations, I noticed seniors often don’t click on unknown buttons, wary of causing an unwanted reaction. Hence all ‘buttons’ in my webapp were launched when they were hovered upon. Furthermore, I discovered seniors understood their computer’s interface best with visual prompts which mirrors their computers so I decided to recreate these windows.

Vocabulary. A whole new user language has developped alongside computers with icons and precise vocabulary. Seniors haven’t integrate this knowledge through trial and error, hindering their ability to comprehend tutorials and seek help. A general knowledge of icons, action terms and the internet is the first step to communicate with computers and other users.

Apprehending basic interfaces. Seniors are often not aware of what each interface enables them to do and where the buttons are situated. Hence, to initiate them to the common and most used interfaces, I mimicked the Google, desktop and file explorer screens with all the necessary pop-up menus.

Quiz. Some users learn best when making error, therefore a quiz was made for the icons. With 3 difficulties, this also helps the users track their progress.
