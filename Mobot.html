Mobot

In 2022, almost 40% of British people said they have done no vigorous exercise, 6 out of 10 citing lack of motivation as their main reason. Mobot was created to mitigate this, motivating you to work out in your home with tailored routines are generated based on your goals.

Mobot

Mobot helps you stay in rhythm during your reps, moving with you with a spring-like movement. Music and lights have also been implemented to help you maintain your motivation throughout the session.

Design Sprint

This project was achieved in a week, during which I reaserched used needs, conducted ideation sessions, refined our concept, created this prototype, filmed and edited a 3 minute video.

Prototyping

Several low-fi prototyping techniques were used, combining different electronic components together to obtain a functional prototype and carboard or paper to achieve the visual appearance.

Video Editing

A 3-minute video explaining our concept and the problem addressed was filmed with the other members of the group, and I edited the different clips together to obtain the result shown below.

Super Quick Prototyping. With anything available.

To effectively convey how Mobot worked in a video, a low-fi but functional prototype had to be created. Many different elements were combined and controled independantly to ensure Mobot would behave properly on camera. An old phone served as a screen and a Mega Pi was remotely controled to turn the motor which created the up and down movement of the Lego structure.
