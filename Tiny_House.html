Tiny House

A carbon neutrality report

Spring 2021

This tiny house was designed using a recycled shipping container as the initial structure. Heat loss and pipe pressure loss was reduced with insulation and appropriate installations. Rain water collection and solar panels complete it’s carbon neutrality.

Creative design ideation and sketching

Desk research for sustainable home features

Technical calculations to validate design

Interior Design. The tiny house was furbished for a couple and aimed to be a holiday house. Multiple large windows, allows the users to enjoy the nature’s the landscape.

Heat loss reduction. Made of metal, the original structure of the container has a high thermal conductivity. Therefore insolation must be added to all surfaces.

The total heat loss of energy is of 701.27 W. This will be counteracted by the underfloor heating mat.

Rain water collection. As the rain flows down the roof, it can be collected and reused. Two users need about 16 790L of grey water per day. This can be covered by UK rainfall with the installation of a 1.08 m² tank.

Carbon Neutrality. To function properly, the tiny house requires 12.92 kW per day, which is equivalent to 7 solar panels’ production. These fit on the roof, making our tiny house carbon neutral.
