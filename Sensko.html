Sensko

An easy donning shoe

Summer 2021

In the midst of covid, our group discovered the challenges runners face during pain treatment. Sensko was designed to enable them to continue their exercise routine without hindering their treatment.

Conducted user interviews, testing and validation

Low-fi and hi-fi prototyping for a refined concept

Smart video editing to showcase final result

Led the project within a group of 3 during Covid

User Interviews. After performing primary and secondary research about the issues runners face, our group found out the greatest challenge to be pain treatment. Indeed, multiple product are available on the market but are often hard to use or are too much of a hassle. Furthermore, most experienced runners experience a runner’s high, thus making it hard for them to stop during a treatment.

Initial creative ideation. How might we ensure and preserve treatment for runners? I came up with a multitude of possible solutions, some very simple, others quite new and complex. However, only 3 were deemed innovative and efficient, thus were taken forward.

Low-fi prototyping with users. Despite the covid lockdown which made it harder to communicate with users, I built several low-fi prototypes aimed to better convey my ideas and collect their feedback. In some case, the runners used items from their homes to experience the concept, and validate, refine or eliminate ideas.

Final user discussion. After multiple exchanges with all users, the best concept was found to be the ‘wrapping shoe’. This shoes’ tongue can be completely opened, which enables the user to place their foot in the shoes without jostling bandages or plasters. The elastic laces allows the shoes to fully adopt the foot’s shape, preventing shifting of the foot within the shoe when exercising.

Hi-fi Prototype for a refined experience validation. Once the initial concept was validated by the user, we regrouped and I built a hi-fi prototype of our idea, using an ordinary sneaker as a base and sewing the different elements accordingly.

User testing. Following the lifting of covid restrictions, we were able to meet up with a few users to test our product. Most were very intrigued and though this concept could be very useful to them. However, some wished to test a more advanced prototype for a longer period to determine how impactful this idea would be.
