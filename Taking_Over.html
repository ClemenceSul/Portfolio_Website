Actor's Creation. From CAD to life.

Actors are a crutial element when narrating a compelling story. In the factory line, all workers were made identical, with very few details, so the sense of seperate individuals disappears and they become a uniform robot-like group drilling into the ice. In comparison, the animals were given a low poly aesthetic granting them a ghost like appearance as phantoms of a past reality.

Design Actor's Movements. Enhancing Realism.

To mimick reality, the cams were designed according to the movements required for each actor. Each worker in the factory line must go up and down to represent drilling. In addition, the cams were designed with a sudden drop to create a constant ticking noise like a machine would. For the penguins, an oscillating motion was prefered, to resemble their walk (top middle). The polar bear and seal used uneven cams for a story-like result of the polar bear reaching for the seal. In the polar bear, the weight of the arm pulled the link up, hence an inversed slider was designed (bottom right).

Final Assembly. A XX piece puzzle.

Although each part of the automaton can designed seperately, all the gears and shafts used the same male / female shapes which made them interchangeable (top left). This was quite useful to test mechanisms with pre-printed pieces and solved multiple issues encountered when assembling. As this project stipulated only 3D printed pieces were allowed, blocks (top right) were designed to avoid unwanted movements of the pieces and some pieces were melted (bottom right) to permenantly attach them.
